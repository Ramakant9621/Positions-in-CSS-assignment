Comparison of Absolute vs Relative Positioning

Property Absolute Positioning Relative Positioning
Position Reference Positioned relative to the nearest positioned ancestor (or the initial containing block if no positioned ancestor). Positioned relative to its normal position in the document flow.
Effect on Document Flow Does not affect the position of other elements; it is taken out of the normal flow. Does not remove the element from the normal flow; other elements will still interact with it.
Top, Right, Bottom, Left Positioned based on the top, right, bottom, or left edges of the nearest positioned ancestor. Positioned based on its normal position in the document flow, then offset according to the specified values.
Example position: absolute; top: 50px; left: 50px; position: relative; top: 50px; left: 50px;
Interaction with Siblings Does not push or affect the position of other siblings. May push or affect the position of other siblings if there is overlap.
